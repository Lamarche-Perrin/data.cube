2.1 Selecting one dimension with select.dim

One first needs to select the dimension of interest using function select.dim. In this example, we focus on the dataset’s temporal dimension, a.k.a. dimension week.

geomedia %>%
    select.dim (week) %>%
    as.data.frame ()
## # A tibble: 79 x 2
##    week       articles
##    <date>        <dbl>
##  1 2015-06-01    5706.
##  2 2015-03-09    6316.
##  3 2014-05-19    5857.
##  4 2014-10-13    6006.
##  5 2014-06-09    5172.
##  6 2015-06-22    6738.
##  7 2015-01-05    5607.
##  8 2014-05-26    5260.
##  9 2015-05-04    6368.
## 10 2014-06-16    3384.
## # … with 69 more rows

The resulting data.cube consists in a unidimensional data structure where variable articles has been aggregated (summed) along dimensions media and country. The resulting data.frame hence gives the total number of published articles corresponding to each element of dimension week.
